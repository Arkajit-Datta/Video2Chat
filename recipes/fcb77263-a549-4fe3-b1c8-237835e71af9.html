Oven-Roasted Garlic Herb Chicken

This recipe features a succulent chicken breast marinated in a garlic herb mixture, pan-seared to golden perfection, and finished in the oven. The dish is accompanied by a rich garlic-infused sauce, making it a hearty and aromatic meal.

Step 1

Prepare the marinade by finely mincing 4 cloves of garlic and mixing them with 1 tablespoon of olive oil, 1 teaspoon of chopped fresh rosemary, 1 teaspoon of chopped fresh thyme, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt.

Step 2

Place two chicken breasts in a bowl and coat them thoroughly with the marinade. Let them sit for at least 30 minutes to absorb the flavors.

Step 3

Preheat the oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.

Step 4

Heat a cast-iron skillet or oven-proof pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, place the marinated chicken breasts skin side down and sear for about 4 minutes until the skin is golden and crispy.

Step 5

Flip the chicken breasts over and cook for another 2 minutes.

Step 6

Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and roast the chicken for 20-25 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165 degrees Fahrenheit.

Step 7

While the chicken is roasting, prepare the garlic sauce by finely chopping 2 additional cloves of garlic. In a small saucepan, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat and saut the garlic until fragrant.

Step 8

Add 1/2 cup of chicken broth to the saucepan, bring to a simmer, and reduce by half. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 9

Once the chicken is done, let it rest for a few minutes before slicing. Serve the chicken with the garlic sauce drizzled on top.
