Spiced Dal Chawal with Pan-Fried Steak

This dish is a fusion of traditional Indian lentils and rice, known as dal chawal, served with a beautifully seasoned and pan-fried steak. The lentils are simmered with aromatic spices, the rice is steamed until fluffy, and the steak is cooked to perfection with a blend of spices that complement the dal. The dish is garnished with fresh cilantro for an added burst of flavor.

Step 1

Rinse 1 cup of lentils and soak them in water for 30 minutes, then drain.

Step 2

In a pot, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, letting them sizzle for a few seconds.

Step 3

Add 1 finely chopped onion to the pot and saut until golden brown.

Step 4

Stir in 2 minced garlic cloves and 1 teaspoon of grated ginger, cooking for another minute.

Step 5

Add the drained lentils to the pot along with 1 teaspoon of turmeric, 1 teaspoon of garam masala, 1/2 teaspoon of red chili powder, and salt to taste.

Step 6

Pour in 4 cups of water, bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the lentils are tender, about 20-25 minutes.

Step 7

While the lentils are cooking, rinse 1 cup of basmati rice until the water runs clear, then soak for 20 minutes before draining.

Step 8

In a separate pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil, add the drained rice and a pinch of salt, and cook until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed, about 15-18 minutes.

Step 9

Season the steak on both sides with a mix of 1 teaspoon of ground coriander, 1/2 teaspoon of cumin, 1/4 teaspoon of turmeric, salt, and pepper.

Step 10

Heat a tablespoon of oil in a pan over medium-high heat and cook the steak for 3-4 minutes on each side for medium-rare, or until desired doneness is reached.

Step 11

Let the steak rest for 5 minutes, then slice it against the grain.

Step 12

Fluff the cooked rice with a fork and serve it on a plate alongside the spiced lentils.

Step 13

Top the dal chawal with slices of the pan-fried steak and garnish with freshly chopped cilantro.
