Daal Chawal with Steak

This dish is a fusion of traditional Indian daal chawal (lentils and rice) paired with a beautifully seasoned and cooked steak. The daal is simmered with aromatic spices, the chawal is steamed to perfection, and the steak is seared to achieve a flavorful crust with a tender interior. It's a comforting and satisfying meal that combines the heartiness of Indian cuisine with the robust flavors of a Western-style steak.

Step 1

Gather all the ingredients: lentils, rice, a steak cut of your choice, onions, garlic, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, garam masala, salt, pepper, cooking oil, and fresh herbs for garnish.

Step 2

Rinse 1 cup of lentils thoroughly and soak them in water for about 30 minutes.

Step 3

In a pot, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, letting them sizzle for a few seconds.

Step 4

Add 1 finely chopped onion and 2 minced garlic cloves to the pot, sauting until they turn golden brown.

Step 5

Drain the lentils and add them to the pot, along with 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of garam masala, and salt to taste. Stir to combine.

Step 6

Pour in 3 cups of water, bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the lentils are tender, about 20-30 minutes.

Step 7

While the daal is cooking, rinse 1 cup of rice until the water runs clear, then soak it for 20 minutes.

Step 8

In another pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil with a pinch of salt. Drain the rice and add it to the boiling water. Cook until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed, about 15-18 minutes.

Step 9

Season the steak with salt and pepper on both sides. Heat a skillet with 1 tablespoon of oil over medium-high heat.

Step 10

Place the steak in the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side for medium-rare, or longer for desired doneness. Let the steak rest for a few minutes before slicing.

Step 11

Check the daal for doneness; the lentils should be soft and the mixture should have a thick consistency. Adjust seasoning if necessary.

Step 12

Fluff the cooked rice with a fork and optionally mix in a teaspoon of ghee or butter for added flavor.

Step 13

Slice the rested steak into strips and serve on top of a bed of rice, accompanied by the daal on the side. Garnish with fresh chopped herbs like cilantro or parsley.
